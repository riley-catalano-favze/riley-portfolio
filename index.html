Methodology

The FAVZE Framework

Everything on this page, search visibility, CRM and automation, copywriting, and social media, runs through one system I built called the FAVZE Framework. It is a five-stage closed-loop lead generation system that treats getting found, building an audience, converting leads, keeping customers, and compounding results as one connected process instead of five separate services.

  • 1
    Full FindabilityMaking a business findable everywhere a lead is searching, across Google, local search, and AI-driven platforms like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini.
  • 2
    Audience ArchitectureBuilding the content, messaging, and social presence that turns that visibility into an engaged, qualified audience.
  • 3
    Velocity InfrastructurePutting the CRM, email automation, and pipeline systems in place so a lead moves quickly and consistently toward conversion.
  • 4
    Zero RecaptureBuilding retention and re-engagement into the system from the start, so leads and customers are never lost to a competitor.
  • 5
    Exponential CompoundingRepurposing and reinforcing every asset across channels so results build on themselves instead of resetting with every new campaign.

FAQ

Ask Me Anything

What makes you a full-stack lead generation digital marketer?
I build the entire lead generation system myself instead of handing off pieces to different specialists. That means search visibility and content to get a business found, CRM and email automation to move leads through the pipeline, and copywriting and social media to keep the brand consistent everywhere a lead encounters it. One person, one connected system, from first search to closed lead.
What is the proudest campaign you have pitched?
I am proudest of pitching "Groundbreakers" for ABI Attachments. It is a strategic user-generated content program that motivates customers to create how-to videos, reviews, and jobsite content with their equipment. The goal is to showcase real-world use, build trust, and improve search and AI-driven visibility through authentic customer stories. The program is now live and in process, which makes it exciting to see it come to life.
What is your specialty?
Making businesses the obvious choice in their local market through Google Business Profile optimization, local SEO, and AI-driven content.
What results have you driven?
I have created thousands of optimized assets including alt text, FAQs, and meta descriptions that improve indexing, rankings, and click-through rates. I focus on work that compounds over time.
Who are your favorite marketers?
Neil Patel and Wes McDowell. Neil Patel's work around SEO and analytics has shaped how I think about growth at scale, while Wes McDowell's approach to messaging and website strategy influences how I turn visibility into conversions.
What is your favorite marketing book?
Purple Cow by Seth Godin. It introduced me to marketing and shaped how I think about standing out instead of blending in.
Favorite marketing podcast?
Marketing School. It is direct, practical, and focused on what actually works.
Favorite marketing YouTuber?
Grow My Ads. I like content that shows exactly how to improve performance, not just talk about it.
Why should someone hire you?
You are not getting layers or handoffs. You are getting someone who builds the strategy and executes it, focused on real visibility and measurable growth.
What is your favorite type of campaign to run?
I love campaigns that combine real customer stories with search and AI strategy. When people see authentic results and the content is optimized for discovery, it hits on trust, visibility, and conversion all at once.
How did you run your review campaign for ABI Attachments?
ABI had website reviews going back to 2016 on WordPress, but the emails were lost when moving to HubSpot. We still had the customer names, so I located them and sent a revised email asking them to leave their review on the new platform. I also reached out to active commenters on Facebook and YouTube. In just two weeks, with only a couple hours of work, this increased their reviews from 52 to 71 and raised their rating from 3.6 to 3.9 stars.
What are you currently learning to grow?
I am focused on strategic category creation and coding in GitHub, all of which I have been building for free to practice and experiment. I am also improving efficiency in WordPress and mastering public relations to maximize reach for public releases.
What platform did you use to build your portfolio?
I built my portfolio using GitHub. It lets me combine design, coding, and content in one place while showcasing everything I have executed and experimented with.
