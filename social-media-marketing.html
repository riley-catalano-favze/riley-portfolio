Strategic Posting Times

Post When Your People Are Actually There

A great post at the wrong hour still underperforms. Before anything goes live, I pull each account's own analytics to find when its specific followers and fans are actually online, since a local Michiana audience and a B2B HR audience do not scroll at the same time or on the same days.

Posting schedules get built around that data, not a generic best-practices chart, and get re-checked regularly since audience behavior shifts as a page grows.

This is also where consistency compounds. Showing up in the same windows, on the same days, trains an algorithm and an audience at the same time. Followers start to expect the post, and the platform starts to expect the engagement, which is what turns a single good post into a pattern of good reach.

Signature Campaign

From Organic Engagement to a Full UGC Program

Case Study · ABI Attachments

Groundbreakers

Groundbreakers started as community management, not a campaign. While running ABI Attachments' YouTube presence, I consistently showed up in the comments, engaging with real conversations instead of posting and disappearing. That consistency was enough for two YouTubers to start creating content featuring ABI equipment entirely on their own, unpaid and unprompted, simply because the engagement made them want to.

That result was the proof of concept. It showed that real engagement could generate content a paid campaign couldn't buy. From there, I built Groundbreakers into a structured user-generated content program that motivates customers to create how-to videos, reviews, and jobsite content with their equipment, turning authentic customer stories into trust signals that improve both search and AI-driven visibility.

The program is now live and actively growing, built entirely on the idea that community management done right becomes its own content engine.

Captions, My Style

I Write for Two Readers at Once

Most people write a caption for the person scrolling. I write it for that person and for the AI model that might be asked the same question later. A caption is not just a caption anymore, it is a small answer sitting in public, waiting to be pulled into a ChatGPT response, a Perplexity result, or a Google AI Overview.

My go-to move is the question-based caption. Opening with the exact question a real person would type into an AI search box mirrors the language those models are already matching against, so the post has a real shot at getting surfaced as the answer. It works every time I use it well, because it is not a trick, it is just writing the caption in the same words the question already exists in.

Landing Pages

Every Campaign Needs Its Own Front Door

A post that performs well and then sends people to a generic homepage is leaving results on the table. I build a dedicated landing page for every real campaign, not just a link in bio pointing to the same place every time.

When the message on the post matches the message on the page word for word, people know they landed in the right place, and they convert instead of bouncing. I've built this exact approach into Favze's own paid social ads page, where every section speaks directly to the offer that brought someone there.

FAQ

Ask Me Anything

What is Groundbreakers?
Groundbreakers is a user-generated content program built for ABI Attachments. It grew out of organic engagement on YouTube, where consistent community management led two YouTubers to start posting content featuring ABI equipment on their own, unpaid and unasked. That became the blueprint for a structured program that motivates customers to create how-to videos, reviews, and jobsite content, building trust and improving visibility across search and AI platforms.
How is Favze's social strategy different from HR Collaboration Group's?
Favze's strategy is built around credibility. As a newer, local, affordable agency, the goal is to look established through educational content and AI-answer-ready posts. HR Collaboration Group is a B2B lead generation business, so its strategy is built around authority and pipeline, reaching HR leaders and business owners through thought leadership that moves them toward a consultation.
Do you need brand photos to create content?
No, but they help. If a brand already has photos or footage, I build the content plan around those assets. If not, I plan and source what's needed to keep the calendar consistent.
Do you write captions for AI or for people?
Both. Every caption is written to read naturally for someone scrolling their feed, while also being structured so AI platforms like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews can understand and surface it.
