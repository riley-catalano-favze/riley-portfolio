The Technical Layer

Schema Markup, With a Real Example

Schema markup is code added to a page that tells Google and AI models exactly what something is, instead of making them guess from plain text. It's the difference between content that might get picked up for a featured snippet and content that's built to be picked up. Below is a real FAQPage schema example, the exact type used to win AEO placements.

{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "How much does furnace repair cost in Goshen, Indiana?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "Furnace repair in Goshen typically runs $150 to $450 depending on the part needed, with the diagnostic fee usually rolled into the final invoice."
      }
    }
  ]
}

"@type": "FAQPage" tells Google this page holds a formal question-and-answer set, not just a paragraph that happens to end in a question mark. That distinction is what makes the content eligible for a featured snippet in the first place.

"mainEntity" is the array of every question on the page. Each question is evaluated independently, so a page with ten well-written entries has ten separate shots at a featured snippet or an AI citation, not one.

"acceptedAnswer" holds the exact text Google or an AI model will lift and display. This is why the writing inside it has to stand completely on its own. It's a finished answer, not a teaser that requires a click to make sense.

Why It Matters for AEO
Google can only feature what it can parse with confidence. Schema removes the guesswork entirely.
Why It Matters for GEO
ChatGPT and Perplexity read structured data the same way search engines do. Clean schema increases the odds of a citation.
Validation Step
Every schema block gets checked against Google's Rich Results Test before it ships live.
Every business page I build carries this same logic across different schema types: LocalBusiness for where and how a business operates, Service for each individual offer, and Person or Organization for entity authority. The FAQPage example above is the one most directly tied to winning AEO placement, which is why it's the one shown in full.

FAQ

Search Visibility Questions

What's the actual difference between SEO, AEO, and GEO?
SEO ranks a page in the top 10 organic results for a keyword. AEO earns the featured snippet or answer box above those results for a specific question. GEO gets a business cited by name inside an AI-generated response from ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI Overviews. They stack: SEO builds the domain authority, AEO earns the answer placement, GEO extends that authority into AI platforms directly.
Do I need Local SEO if I already have SEO?
Yes, if the business serves a specific geographic area. Standard SEO competes for a keyword nationally. Local SEO is judged on relevance, proximity, and prominence within a specific market, and determines whether a business shows up in the Local 3-Pack and Maps results.
What is AI Optimization, specifically?
AI Optimization is the technical foundation underneath AEO and GEO: schema markup, entity signals, and content structure that let AI models parse a page and cite it confidently. Without it, even well-written AEO and GEO content has nothing structurally trustworthy to stand on.
What tools do you actually use for this work?
Semrush for keyword research, technical audits, and AI visibility tracking. Google Search Console for indexing and performance data. AnswerThePublic and AlsoAsked for mapping the exact questions customers are asking, which feeds directly into AEO content and FAQ structuring.
How does copywriting connect to SEO?
SEO tells you where to put a keyword. Copywriting decides whether the sentence around that keyword is worth reading, and increasingly, worth an AI model citing as an answer. I built my SEO skill set on top of a copywriting foundation, not the other way around.
